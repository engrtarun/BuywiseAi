PART 1: HACKATHON PAGE SUBMISSION TEXT
================================================================================

--- PROJECT SUMMARY ---
BuyWise AI is a conversational shopping assistant that replaces the traditional online-shopping workflow — searching multiple platforms, comparing prices, reading dozens of reviews — with a single, highly interactive natural-language conversation. 

Unlike standard shopping tools that simply sort database tables by price or star ratings, BuyWise AI utilizes a specialized AI reasoning layer to understand vague, unstructured human intent, conduct a structured intake interview, pull live multi-retailer products, and present them in beautiful, custom-designed interactive widgets. 

Additionally, the platform includes a gamified "Virtual Wardrobe" that lets users mix and match saved apparel in Swipe or Canvas (Drag-and-Drop) modes, using a Gemini-powered "Match Meter" to evaluate styling scores and outfit synergy.

--- THE PROBLEM ---
Online shopping today is a chore. Consumers experience "choice paralysis" and "review fatigue." When searching for a product, they must:
1. Open 4+ browser tabs to check different retailers (Amazon, Flipkart, Myntra, etc.).
2. Sift through dozens of sponsored and SEO-optimized listings that do not match their actual needs.
3. Read hundreds of reviews, often written by bots or containing contradictory advice.
4. Calculate sizing and compatibility manually, resulting in high return rates (up to 30% in apparel) and transaction abandonment.

Current e-commerce platforms are optimized for clicks and page views (advertising models) rather than helping the user make a fast, correct decision.

--- THE SOLUTION ---
BuyWise AI collapses this fragmented workflow into a conversational assistant. Instead of rendering a wall of plain text links like standard chatbots, it acts as a digital personal shopping consultant that:
- Conducts structured qualifying interviews to lock down specific use cases and budgets.
- Scrapes and normalizes live, real-time retail data across multiple vendors.
- Reranks results using a reasoning engine that favors review counts, reliability, and custom user filters rather than sponsored listings.
- Provides interactive UI widgets (carousels, sizing forms, and drag-and-drop canvases) to build outfits and calculate fits dynamically.

--- TARGET AUDIENCE ---
1. Time-Poor Shoppers: Busy professionals who want the absolute best option within their budget without spending 3 hours researching.
2. Gen-Z & Millennial Fashion Shoppers: Users who want visual, gamified outfit planning and instant AI styling feedback before buying.
3. Multi-Member Households: Families who shop for multiple people and need to manage distinct sizing/budget profiles under a single account.

PART 2: THE ULTIMATE DEBATE: "WHY NOT JUST USE CHATGPT?"
================================================================================
Critics and investors often ask: "Why can't I just ask ChatGPT to find me a product?" 
BuyWise AI is fundamentally different. Here is the technical and experiential breakdown of why ChatGPT is not built for this, and how BuyWise AI proves them wrong.

1. INTERACTIVE UI/UX VS. CHATGPT'S "WALL OF TEXT"
   - ChatGPT's output is limited to raw text, markdown bullets, and simple hyperlinked lists. The user has to read massive paragraphs, copy-paste data, and do all the cognitive labor.
   - BuyWise AI uses structured JSON output schemas dynamically mapped to rich, interactive frontend components. If the AI recommends shoes, they are rendered in a horizontal, swipeable product carousel. Clarifying questions are presented as tappable select-chips and budget sliders. A clothing combination is rendered as a spatial moodboard canvas. The user never feels like they are reading an essay — they are interacting with an application.

2. STRUCTURED SHOPPING CONVERSATION LOGIC
   - ChatGPT is a generalist. If you ask it for a laptop, it will dump whatever is in its training data (which is static) or do a generic web search. It does not qualify your needs systematically.
   - BuyWise AI implements a strict, multi-turn state machine:
     * Explore Mode: Optimizes for instant, low-stakes browsing.
     * Deep Research Mode: Enforces a strict qualification workflow. It is programmatically blocked from recommending items until it has qualified: (1) Use-case/specs, and (2) Budget. 
     * It also forces the selection of a "Stretch Option" (10-15% above budget) to show what features are unlocked with a slight budget increase.

3. REAL-TIME RETAILED-DATA INGESTION AND SCHEMA NORMALIZATION
   - ChatGPT has static training cutoffs, and its web search returns unstructured HTML articles. It cannot compare actual retail inventory.
   - BuyWise AI integrates with real-time shopping APIs (Serper / Google Shopping) and passes raw search data through a custom Normalization Layer (`normalizeProduct.ts`). This layer unifies disparate vendor formats (prices, ratings, image dimensions from Amazon, Flipkart, Myntra, Zomato, Blinkit, etc.) into a unified schema so the AI reasons over true, live inventory.

4. RELIABILITY RERANKING VS. RATING MANIPULATION
   - ChatGPT reads a product rating of "4.9 stars" and takes it at face value.
   - BuyWise AI's reasoning layer (`writer.ts`) is explicitly prompt-engineered to evaluate review COUNT alongside review score. It prioritizes a 4.4-star item with 5,000 reviews over a 4.9-star item with 3 reviews, weeding out SEO-hacked and bot-reviewed listings.

5. THE MULTI-PROFILE PROFILE WIZARD
   - ChatGPT does not remember your size, gender, style preference, or budget from session to session.
   - BuyWise AI has a Netflix-style Multi-Profile Switcher (up to 4 profiles). If you switch to "Sister", it automatically injects her shoe size (e.g., UK 5), her preferred category, and budget caps into the conversational context. The AI knows what to find without you typing it out every time.

6. SPATIAL OUTSIDE INTERACTION: THE VIRTUAL WARDROBE
   - ChatGPT cannot help you visualize outfit combinations.
   - BuyWise AI has a dedicated Virtual Wardrobe workspace. Users can use Swipe Mode (mix-and-match deck with Framer Motion animations) or Canvas Mode (drag-and-drop moodboard via `@hello-pangea/dnd`) to lay out tops and bottoms. They can get a live price tally of the outfit and trigger the "Match Meter" — a low-latency, text-based Gemini API call that rates their style combination and returns a savage, witty styling review.

7. ENTERPRISE-GRADE API RESILIENCY & LOAD-BALANCING
   - A commercial app relying solely on a single API client is vulnerable to rate limits and downtime.
   - BuyWise AI is built with a Dual-AI Engine with Failover. Its primary reasoning runs on Gemini 2.5 Flash. If Gemini throws a 429 Rate Limit error, the app automatically and transparently falls back to Groq running Llama 3.1. It also implements a round-robin `keyManager.ts` to cycle through multiple API keys to maximize uptime.

PART 3: THE 4-STEP HACKATHON PRESENTATION SEQUENCE
================================================================================

1. WHY THE SOLUTION IS RELEVANT (The Problem & Context)
   - Choice overload: The average shopper visits 4-5 sites and reads 20+ reviews before clicking "Buy".
   - Time waste: E-commerce search engines are broken, filled with sponsored listings that prioritize advertiser profit over user satisfaction.
   - Sizing mismatch: Up to 30% of online fashion purchases are returned due to fit issues, costing retailers billions.

2. HOW IT WILL SOLVE THE PROBLEM (The Mechanism)
   - Guided intake: Conversation acts as a filter. Instead of typing complex searches, the AI guides the user to reveal what matters.
   - Multi-retailer aggregation: Gathers inventory from all stores in one place.
   - Intelligent Reranking: Filters products based on actual value (review counts, budget compliance, specifications) rather than advertising bids.

3. HOW IT WILL CREATE VALUE (The Value Proposition)
   - For Users: Saves hours of research; eliminates return headaches via profile sizing alignment; provides fun, interactive wardrobe styling.
   - For Retailers: Increases conversion rates by delivering high-intent buyers directly to checkout; lowers return rates by pre-qualifying sizing.
   - Monetization: Affiliate link commissions; premium subscription tiers for "Deep Research Mode"; B2B white-labeling for retailers.

4. WHAT OUR SOLUTION DOES (Product & Features)
   - Explore Mode: Quick search and interactive product carousels.
   - Deep Research Mode: Qualifies user needs step-by-step; recommends a primary hero pick, secondary backups, and an analytical verdict.
   - Quick Buy Multi-Profile System: Switch shopper profiles (up to 4) instantly, auto-binding sizing and budgets.
   - Virtual Wardrobe: Drag-and-drop outfit canvas, swipeable fashion deck, and AI "Match Meter" styling score.
